About Noses

In traditional Chinese physiognomy, the nose is one of the most important organs of the face. A physiognomist will use the nose to determine a person's heart, personality and fortune.

The upper part of the nose between the eyes is called the "Shan Gen" or nasion, and a person with a thick nasion is considered to be energetic, while the opposite is considered to be physically weak.

The middle part of the nose is called the "Nian Shang" or bridge of the nose. When there is a raised bone in the bridge of the nose, the person is considered to be strong and irritable. A person with a low nose bridge is considered to have a bad marriage.

The lower part of the nose is called the “BiTou" or pronasale, and a person with a soft pronasale is considered to be less fortunate than a person with a hard pronasale.
